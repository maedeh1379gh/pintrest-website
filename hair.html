Why Do You Have to Spend a Lot of Money on Your Curly Hair?

Introduction: As someone with curly hair, I know that each year, I spend a significant amount of money on my hair just to look good and avoid frizz. In this article, I’m going to talk about what I do and what I use.

1.Oil: Before washing my hair, I apply some oil (depending on the day of the week) and let it sit for about an hour. It helps my hair become smoother and shinier when it's dry, but I don’t do it every time before showering—usually just once a week. If you're wondering what kind of oil is good for your hair, I would recommend coconut , argan, and rosemary. However, it ultimately depends on your hair type, as different oils may work better for different people. For example, coconut oil makes my hair really dry, while it works perfectly for my friend.

2.Shampoo: Some people shower once a week, while others do it every day, and I think it's something that depends on someone's hair type rather than just science. For me, washing every other day works best. In the shower, I wash my hair twice: first with a shampoo designed for curly hair, and then with sulfate-free shampoo . also using sulfate-free shampoo helps you with hair loss. An important thing to note is that you shouldn't wash your hair with hot water, as it can be really damaging.

3.Conditioner: After washing my hair, I use conditioner and brush my hair at the same time. I usually leave it on for around 5 minutes, but if I'm using a treatment conditioner , I leave it on for 20 to 30 minutes. Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so incorporating a deep conditioning treatment into your routine is crucial. Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish and strengthen your curls, leaving them soft, smooth, and more manageable.

5.Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt: Please don’t use the same towel that you dry your body with for your hair. Your hair needs something soft to be touched with right after the shower, so be gentler with it. Regular towels can cause frizz and breakage, whereas microfibre towels or t-shirts are gentler on the hair, helping to maintain the integrity of your curls

6.Gel: Using gel is really essential. Why? It lets your curls stay in place for a longer time and keeps the products that you used for your hair intact, making them last longer on your hair.

6.Diffuser: If you have curly hair and don’t have a diffuser at home, I recommend you stop reading and just order one because it's a game-changer for your hair. A diffuser helps to evenly distribute heat and airflow, reducing frizz and enhancing the natural texture of your curls. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your curls with the diffuser to create volume and definition.

Conclusion: With the right products and techniques, caring for curly hair can be a breeze. Invest in high-quality products that cater to the specific needs of your curls, and don't be afraid to experiment to find what works best for you. With a little love and attention, you can embrace your curls and rock your natural texture with confidence.
